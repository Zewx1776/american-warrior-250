# American Warrior Campaign — Landing Page Copy Context
> Use this file as context when prompting Claude to generate copy for the American Warrior landing page. This is a product collection page featuring patches and t-shirts tied to the Swampfox Optics 250th anniversary campaign.

---

## Campaign Overview

**Campaign Name:** American Warrior

**Brand:** Swampfox Optics

**Campaign Theme:** A limited edition apparel and accessories collection celebrating America's 250th anniversary. The campaign pays tribute to the lineage of American warriors — from the minutemen of 1776 to the operators of the Global War on Terror — through bold, historically-rooted graphic design.

**Core Message:** The tools of war change. The spirit does not. Two and a half centuries of American lethality, remembered and worn.

**Tone:** Confident, unapologetic, historically grounded, patriotic without being saccharine. Speaks directly to veterans, gun owners, and American heritage enthusiasts. Not corporate — this is edge-of-the-flagpole copy.

**Audience:** Veterans, active military, gun owners, competitive shooters, American heritage/history enthusiasts, Second Amendment community.

**Things to Avoid:** Soft, generic patriotism. Overclaiming. Anything that sounds like a department store "Americana" campaign. Do not sanitize the military themes.

Celebrate the rebellious roots of the American spirit with this PVC patch. This design is a tactical riff on Benjamin Franklin's famous 1754 political cartoon. ## Landing Page Copy Guidelines

**Hero Section:**
- Lead with the campaign identity, not a single product. The opening should feel like a declaration, not a product listing.
- Suggested angle: 250 years. Every war. One spirit.

**Product Section Headers:**
- Group by design series (American Warrior, GWOT, Uncle FAFO, Join Or Find Out) rather than by product type (all shirts, then all patches).
- Show the shirt and patch together within each series grouping.

**CTAs:**
- Primary: "Shop Now" or "Get Yours"
- Secondary where applicable: "View the Full Collection"
- Keep CTAs direct and action-oriented — no "discover" or "explore" language.

**Historical References to Use:**
- 1776 / Revolution / Rattlesnake / Don't Tread on Me
- French and Indian War / Benjamin Franklin / "Join, or Die" 1754
- World War I / James Montgomery Flagg / 1917 Uncle Sam poster
- Post-9/11 / GWOT / Afghanistan / Iraq
- Minutemen, doughboys, G.I.s, operators — the full lineage

**Phrases Worth Preserving from Product Copy:**
- "Two and a half centuries of lethality"
- "The tools change but the spirit remains the same"
- "The first and last colony to win independence from a global empire"
- "Sand, sweat, and steel"
- "The American spirit is hard to kill"
- "Liberty is not a suggestion. It is a promise backed by consequences."
- "The colonial beginnings of the Swampfox spirit"
